# Interpretability Papers

## 2019-ICML-Towards A Deep and Unified Understanding of Deep Neural Models in NLP

Recently, quantitatively explaining intermediate layers of a
DNN has attracted increasing attention, especially in computer
vision.

- 2014-ICLR-Deep Inside Convolutional Networks- Visualising Image Classification Models and Saliency Maps
- 2014-ECCV-Visualizing and Understanding Convolutional Networks
- 2016-KDD-LIME-Why should i trust you? Explaining the predictions of any classifier
- 2017-CVPR-Network Dissection: Quantifying Interpretability of Deep Visual Representations
- 2018-AAAI-Interpreting CNN Knowledge Via An Explanatory Graph
- 2018-arXiv-Unsupervised Learning of Neural Networks to Explain Neural Networks
- 2019-CVPR-Interpreting CNNs via Decision Trees

A key task in this direction is to associate latent representations with the interpretable input units (e.g., image pixels or words) by measuring the contribution or saliency of the inputs. Existing methods can be grouped into three major categories:

1. **Gradient-based**
  - [2016-NAACL-Visualizing and Understanding Neural Models in NLP](./Interpretability_Papers/2016-NAACL-Visualizing and Understanding Neural Models in NLP.pdf)
  - [2017-ICCV-Interpretable Explanations of Black Boxes by Meaningful Perturbation](./Interpretability_Papers/2017-ICCV-Interpretable Explanations of Black Boxes by Meaningful Perturbation.pdf)
  - [2017-ICML-Axiomatic Attribution for Deep Networks](./Interpretability_Papers/2017-ICML-Axiomatic Attribution for Deep Networks.pdf)
2. **Inversion-based**
  - [2018-KDD-Towards Explanation of DNN-based Prediction with Guided Feature Inversion](./Interpretability_Papers/2018-KDD-Towards Explanation of DNN-based Prediction with Guided Feature Inversion.pdf)
3. methods that utilize **layer-wise relevance propagation (LRP)**
  - [2016-ACL-Explaining Predictions of Non-Linear Classifiers in NLP](./Interpretability_Papers/2016-ACL-Explaining Predictions of Non-Linear Classifiers in NLP.pdf)

These methods have demonstrated that quantitative explanations for intermediate layers can enrich our understanding about the inner working mechanism of a model, such as, the roles of neurons.
